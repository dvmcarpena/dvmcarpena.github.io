--- title: "Grothendieck's homotopy hypothesis" event: "Fall 2021 seminar of the Topology Group at Universitat de Barcelona" location: "Barcelona, Spain" date: 2021-10-25 language: en ---

In higher category theory, \(\infty\)-groupoids are \(\infty\)-categories whose morphisms are weakly invertible at all orders. Every topological space has an associated \(\infty\)-groupoid, named its fundamental \(\infty\)-groupoid, which encodes the information of higher paths over the space. The statement that every space can be recovered up to homotopy from its fundamental \(\infty\)-groupoid is known as Grothendieckâ€™s homotopy hypothesis. In this talk we present the model of \(\infty\)-categories based on topologically enriched categories, and discuss the homotopy hypothesis in this context.
